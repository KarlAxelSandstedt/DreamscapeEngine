----- Velocity solver + Position solver (PGS_NGS) -----

Why? Because ordinary PGS + velocity steering seem unstable, especially when stacking
boxes. As we solve the contacts for a box, to much force is applied at one contact 
point in relation to rest, so contact digs deeper at the other contact points, while
one contact point is removed. This results in the contact flipping from a full, flat
rectangle surface to a triangle surface and back again, over and over, resulting in
the "wooble effect" of the box stack.

One approach to solve this (or an extension to PGS_NGS), is to solve a set of related
contacts at once, instead of one-by-one. This is more costly, but the result is that 
the solution takes all contact constraints into account at the same. We skip this 
technique, called block solving, for now, and only consider NGS.
